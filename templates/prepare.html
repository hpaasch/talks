10 Tips for acquiring public information

  1. Don't be a jerk. Follow the rules. Be flexible about format and delivery method.
  2. Be specific, not overly broad.
  3. Figure out what data actually exists. No analyzing or creating of new records.
  4. Learn the collection method, so you can match it. (If you know they'll search emails for keywords, suggest the keywords.)
  5. FOIA the FOIAs. FOIA the fields. FOIA the talking points. FOIA the database layout.
  6. Find the official right person, and also the unofficial person who actually knows. Make a verbal request. Surface the concerns.
  7. If you believe a certain record exists, describe it in your request.
  8. Always cite the statute. Always keep a log of communication.
  9. Be reasonable about the time period.
  10. Copy someone on your request. Department head, and PIO. Your lawyer. Elected official.

Worth noting:

  • Submission preferences vary: mail, email, special form.
  • Sometimes they impose a fee. Request a waiver, if you have an agrument for that.
  • Redaction -- the whole record can't be denied if just one column is not public.
