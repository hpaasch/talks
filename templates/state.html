Understanding North Carolina public records

Worth noting

  • Not as formal in designating federal FOIA contacts. The public official in charge of an office is the "custodian."
  • No "deliberative process" exemption for memos as in federal FOIA.
  • Attorney general can advise and mediate, but not enforce.
  • Every public agency is required to have an index of databases.

Databases

  • list of data fields
  • a description of the format or record layout
  • information on frequency of updates
  • a list of any data fields to which public access is restricted
  • a description of each form in which the database can be copied or reproduced
  • schedule of fees for making copies in each form

Money

  • Request an estimate in writing before agreeing.
  • Actual cost of copies is allowed, including digital.
  • Extra staff time for tech.
  • Can charge if they're going to create or compile records that don't exist.
  • You can appeal the cost estimate.
  • Government cannot charge to separate comingled data.

Some exemptions

  • Complete personnel files are generally exempt. But some info is public, such as name, age and salary.
  • Confidential communications between attorneys and their government clients.
  • Some state and local income tax information
  • Trade secrets
  • Detailed criminal investigation records. SBI is completely exempt.
  • 911 database of phone numbers, locations, names
  • Autopsy photos
  • Sensitive public security information
  • Industrial or Business expansion while in negotiation
